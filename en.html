// qmetrika_philosophy

Capital Q — operating model

A token spent and forgotten is expense. A token spent, governed, and captured is capital. Eight principles that are architectural decisions, not aspirations.

P1

The token is capital, not expense

Every foundation model spend must leave a deposit: a label, an evaluation, a fine-tuned model. If consumed without capturing anything, it was pure cost.

P2

Measure local vs foundation

The ratio of local to rented work is the health metric. More local = more margin, more autonomy, more sovereignty.

P3

Control lives in the tool

The decision about what gets executed is deterministic code at the gate, not LLM judgment. Fail-closed by design.

P4

Capture human judgment

Every expert approval is recorded, labeled, and reusable. The label is AI's most expensive input; here it is a byproduct of the work.

P5

Audit from day one

Verifiable chain of every action. Not just compliance (GDPR, EU AI Act): it is the evaluation dataset that no generic can rent.

P6

Narrow and deep

The vertical wedge is where capital compounds. One agent excellent at a specific task beats a mediocre one that tries everything.

P7

The moat is the data, not the weights

Models expire; captured judgment and your domain's evaluations do not. A better model only makes your capital yield more.

P8

Design before you spend

The Canvas before the code. Ambiguity is paid for in burned tokens. Resolve it first, on paper, where it's cheap.
