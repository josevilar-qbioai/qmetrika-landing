The interruption that breaks the pattern

In repeat-expansion disorders, age at onset is set not only by how many repeats you carry but by whether the tract is interrupted. energoRNA measures it with a single physical observable — the periodicity loss of the stacking profile — that predicts onset better than counting. From point variant to structured RNA

The QMetrika thermodynamic engine was born for point variants: the nearest-neighbor stacking energy profile of the mRNA. There we showed that the synonymous pathogenicity signal lives in the local stacking, not in secondary structure. That distinction is exactly the hinge into repeat-expansion disorders: in a point variant, perturbing global structure is noise; in an expansion, the periodic structure of the repeat is the relevant entity. The same local physics, in a new regime.

Counting repeats isn't enough

Myotonic dystrophy (DM1), Huntington and the ataxia SCA1 share one clinical fact: age at onset correlates with repeat length, but weakly. The real modifier is interruptions — variant units embedded in the tract (CCG in the DM1 CTG, the CAA codon in the Huntington CAG, CAT in SCA1). They reduce somatic instability and delay onset. The problem: the clinical observable remains a scalar, a count, blind to the pattern.

The observable: periodicity loss

A pure repeat produces a periodic stacking profile. An interruption breaks that pattern locally. energoRNA quantifies it with a single number — the loss of autocorrelation (at the motif period) of the Turner profile:

# periodicity_loss = 1 − ACF_period(Φ)
pip install energorna
energorna score --repeat CTG --n 40

It auto-calibrates the period per motif (3 for trinucleotides, 4 for the CCTG tetranucleotide of DM2) and, unlike counting, is sensitive to the type of interruption (CCG disrupts the pattern far more than CTC) and to its position in the tract.

It's not how many repeats there are, it's the break of the periodic pattern of the energy field. The same physical observable, in DM1, Huntington and SCA1.

An honest correlate — and deployable

Honest framing: periodicity loss is a biologically motivated correlate —the periodicity of the stacking profile is a proxy for the contiguous slipped-strand substrate on which the repair machinery (MMR) drives somatic expansion— not a demonstrated cause. It generates falsifiable predictions (correlation with measured somatic instability, UV melting, MSH3 binding). And because it is a physical scalar with no deep network, it is deployable: numpy-only, no GPU, auditable.
